Family Therapy

Healing happens when we heal the system

Marriage and family therapy evolved from 1930s one-on-one sessions. Therapists noticed clients presented similar symptoms across families.

Pioneers like Murray Bowen began inviting parents into therapy. With parents present, children's symptoms reduced dramatically.

This led to research at Milan's Palo Alto Institute studying family influence on child behavior. Cybernetics science showed how parts of a system affect each other. The family IS a system.

Four major models emerged:

• Bowen Theory from psychoanalysis
• Structural & Strategic from behaviorism
• Human Validation Process from humanistic therapy

At 4 Supports Therapy, we've studied all four models in depth - examining key concepts, family dysfunction patterns, therapy goals, therapist roles, and techniques for each.

Building on this foundation, I've developed my own integrated model of marriage and family therapy.
