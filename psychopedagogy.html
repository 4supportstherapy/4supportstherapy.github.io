Psychopedagogy

Learning That Heals. Teaching That Transforms.

Just like counselling, teaching is not just standing in front of a class and talking. It is more: it is about disseminating information with the clear aim of learning taking place.

That means someone can talk for hours, or minutes, depending on class time — and unfortunately, teaching and learning can still fail to take place. For learning to truly happen, the teacher needs to use a deliberate set of skills and strategies.

Effective learning happens when Knowledge, Skills, and the right Attitudes (KSA) are acquired. For this to happen, the teacher must draw from proven Learning Frameworks:

Key Learning Frameworks We Use:

• Humanistic Theories - Maslow’s Hierarchy: learners must feel safe and valued before they learn
• David Kolb’s Experiential Learning - learning through cycles of doing, reflecting, and applying
• Cognitive Theories of Learning - understanding how the brain processes information
• Howard Gardner’s Multiple Intelligences - recognizing 8 ways students learn best
• Bloom’s Taxonomy - moving learners from remembering to creating

Our Theoretical Framework

This approach is grounded in a blended framework that integrates classical learning theories and contemporary inclusivity models. This reflects today’s classrooms: inclusivity spans pedagogy, curriculum, and school policy.

Primary Guiding Frameworks:

1. Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
We proactively design instruction for learner variability — offering multiple ways to engage, understand, and show competence. Students can use oral, visual, or hands-on tasks, not just written tests.

2. Inclusive Pedagogy Model
We treat diversity as the norm. Teachers design tasks where ALL students participate meaningfully — adapting groups so every ability contributes.

3. Policy Implementation Theory
Teachers are “street-level bureaucrats” who interpret national policies. This explains why curriculum goals vary across schools — and why training matters.

Supporting Theories:

Vygotsky - Social interaction shapes learning | Piaget - Match teaching to cognitive stage | Bruner - Scaffolding support that fades | Kolb - Do, reflect, apply cycles | Gardner - Honor 8 intelligences

Our 3-Level Model: Inclusivity as an Ecosystem

1. Pedagogical Level - Differentiated instruction guided by inclusive pedagogy

2. Curricular Level - Engagement strategies from UDL, experiential learning, multiple intelligences

3. Policy & Leadership Level - How school leaders interpret and apply national frameworks

This model ensures inclusivity is not isolated classroom tricks, but systemic alignment of pedagogy, curriculum, and leadership — creating schools that deliver equitable outcomes for all learners.
